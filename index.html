History

The Brabant horse, also known as the Belgian Heavy Breed, originated in Belgium during the medieval period. Bred for its power and gentle temperament, it was used for agricultural work, especially heavy haulage and field labor due to its muscular build and strong hindquarters.

Physical Traits

The Brabant draft horse is a large, muscular breed, typically standing between 16 and 17 hands tall. Known for its strength, docile temperament, and willingness to work, the Brabant is a highly intelligent breed, making it easy to train and work with.

Coat Colors

Brabants come in a variety of colors, including chestnut, bay, grey, flaxen, bay roan, and blue roan. Their stunning coat colors, combined with their muscular build, give them a distinctive and often kind appearance.

Conservation

During World War II, the Brabant breed faced near-extinction due to disruptions in traditional breeding practices. Fortunately, efforts from organizations like Trekpaard.net helped preserve the breed, and today, while still relatively rare, Brabants are no longer near-extinct.
